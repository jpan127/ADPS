About ADPS

With the surge in popularity of autonomous vehicles and the recent boom in machine learning research, tedious and manual jobs are gradually being replaced by more efficient and less error-prone robotic systems. A prime example of a task that can be replaced is the mail delivery responsibility. We propose an autonomous postal delivery system that can adapt to its surroundings and safely and efficiently deliver mail to the correct addresses.

Despite the simplicity of this task, there are a myriad of issues with manually delivering mail. Our concept will specifically target three areas of interest: accuracy and reliability, reducing manual labor and poor work environments, and speed through automation. Manual delivery has a chance for human error: packages can go missing, packages can be delivered to the wrong address. Often times, trusting a human is far less predictable than trusting a strictly designed machine. Having a human walk around the neighborhood to each house can be a labor-intensive task. Especially in areas with extreme high or low temperatures, being exposed to harsh weathers can be detrimental to worker’s health and ultimately inefficient. Humans are inherently limited by their energy and physical capability. As can be seen with all industries gradually being automated, machines and artificial intelligence excel at what inherently limits humans.

Theoretically, well-designed software can achieve higher accuracy than any human can. Our robot will be designed void of human mistakes and will ensure delivery of every package to every designated address. The system will also be designed with resiliency to withstand the varying elements of weather. It will be efficient, consistent, and easily deployable to any arbitrary neighborhood. Robots can be configured by their hardware/software and extended with longer lasting batteries and faster motors. The system shall be able to autonomously traverse its designated target area and report back to the master’s location. The concept is envisioned to be able to mass deploy units to swiftly cover vast areas and completely automize the postal delivery industry.
